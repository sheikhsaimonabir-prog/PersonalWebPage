Design of A 3kW Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine for Prospecctive Locations of Bangladesh

Degree: B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering

University: Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology

Year: 2019

Supervisor: Dr. Mazharul Islam

Thesis Partner: AUST

Abstract

Wind energy is one of the emerging renewable energy sources in Bangladesh. In a prospective site, a significant amount of energy can be harnessed from wind. However, it is very critical to conduct a rigorous analysis of available wind resources before undertaking any wind power project. Over the years, many wind resource assessment projects have been undertaken in different locations in Bangladesh and researchers have found good potentiality of wind for energy production. Keeping this in mind, in this research a wind resource assessment has been made for nine prospective locations of Bangladesh with the help of Weibull distribution. Two important Weibull parameters such as Weibull shape factor “k” and Weibull scale factor “c” have been calculated for different methods numerically using a highly functional programming language called Python. Besides a 3kW horizontal axis wind turbine blade having a rotor diameter of 3meter is also designed using an open-source, cross-platform simulation software called QBlade. Several blade designs using different types of NACA series airfoils and NREL (National Renewable Energy Laboratory) suggested airfoils have been made to get the best performing wind turbine blade. Then blade performance has been analyzed comparatively. From the analysis, the best-performing blade design has been chosen for the design of a 3kW horizontal axis wind turbine. Later a simulation has been conducted using QBlade software for determining annual energy yield for nine prospective locations in Bangladesh. Results are showing that Inani and Parkay is the most prospective area for wind energy production compared to other locations. It is also found that blade design with NACA 4818 shows better performance in terms of starting torque & airfoil thickness and NACA 4415 & NACA 4615 in terms of Cp value. So, it can be hoped that this research will help the project developers to find the prospective location for planting wind power plants in Bangladesh.

Objectives

The objectives of this thesis are:

1. Determine Weibull shape and scale parameters for selected locations in Bangladesh using diversified methods

2. Design a smaller-capacity horizontal axis wind turbine for the prospective locations of Bangladesh

3. Determine the annual energy yield from designed turbine for selected prospective locations in Bangladesh

Methodology

  • Collected wind data (40 m height) for 9 locations in Bangladesh from NREL database (2014–2017)
  • Estimated Weibull parameters (k, c) using four methods: PDM, AMLM, SDM, MEPFM
  • Processed data and performed calculations using Python (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib)
  • Selected airfoils (NREL S-series and NACA series) for blade design
  • Developed blade geometry based on established experimental data
  • Designed and simulated turbine blades using QBlade (BEM theory)
  • Validated results using reference experimental Cp–TSR data
  • Calculated annual energy production using Weibull parameters and turbine simulation

    • Tools Used

  • Python (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)
  • QBlade (aerodynamic design and simulation)
  • Weibull statistical models
  • NREL wind data platform (RE Explorer)

    • Results

  • Weibull parameters varied across locations, with k ≈ 1.3 – 2.3 and c ≈ 3.3 – 5.36 m/s, indicating moderate wind potential
  • Best wind resources identified at Inani and Parkay Beach with highest scale factors (~5.3 m/s)
  • Validation achieved close agreement with experimental data (Cp ≈ 0.45 at TSR ≈ 10)
  • Among airfoils, NACA 4818 showed strong starting torque and thickness performance
  • NACA 4415 and 4615 demonstrated better aerodynamic efficiency (higher Cp)
  • Annual energy yield analysis confirmed feasibility of small-scale wind turbines in selected coastal regions
